# Hardware Learning Log 🔩

> Documenting my journey into Arduino, embedded systems, and hardware prototyping — driven by a mission to build low-cost, tangible assistive technology for Persons with Visual Impairment in India.

---

## Why Hardware?

Working in the accessibility field in India puts one uncomfortable truth in front of you fast — **less than 0.5% of Persons with Visual Impairment (PVI) in our country have access to assistive technology systems**, let alone the ability to use them effectively.

I had been working on the AI and software side of this problem. Powerful models, elegant pipelines — but out of reach for the people who need them most.

Hardware changed the way I think about solutions. A tangible device that costs a few hundred rupees, runs without internet, and fits in a pocket is not a compromise. For most PVI in India, **it is the only viable solution**.

The complete team at [NCAHT, IIT Delhi](https://ncaht.in) is on a mission to empower as many PVI as possible. My short-term contribution is learning to build things that can actually be held, carried, and used everyday.

Arduino has been the most fun thing I have learned so far. The possibilities that hardware opens up are genuinely amazing. 🚴✨

---
## The Bigger Picture

This is not just a learning log. Every experiment here is a step toward building integrated systems where AI meets affordable hardware; solutions that work in the real world, for real people, without requiring a smartphone, a subscription, or a stable internet connection.

If that mission resonates with you, feel free to reach out or collaborate.
